Solent-Modules

Modules

ID Name Semester Description
COM709 Computer Fundamentals 1 The module introduces computer fundamentals and related concepts. A broad background to the history of computing will be covered beginning with computer organisation, functioning, and architecture then focusing on number systems, as well as components, and their performance, computational methods, and novel concepts and solutions.
COM712 Networking 1 This module is an introduction to networks and is designed to develop the skills necessary to plan and implement small networks across a range of applications. The module considers the basic design of a network infrastructure and addresses issues arising from the pre-installation and implementation stages of its development. It also considers network configuration, troubleshooting, monitoring and support issues. By the end of the module, students will be able to build simple LANs, perform basic configurations for routers and switches, and implement IP addressing schemes.
MAA103 Research Methods 1 Throughout this module, we will first delve into the fundamental philosophies and concepts that underpin research, providing you with a solid foundational understanding. Subsequently, we will explore the core methodological approaches that serve as the bedrock for rigorous research practices. Moving beyond theory, we will then embark on a detailed exploration of specific research methods, each of which is a vital component of robust research endeavours.
COM716 Security & Ethical Hacking 1 Ethical hacking is one of the core specialisations in Cybersecurity. This module provides the theoritical and practical background which would allow students pursue a career in Ethical Hacking and other associated Cybersecurity specialisations. This module is based mainly on the official EC Council Certified Ethical Hacker v12 Certification training.
COM713 Cyber Security Application 2 Cybersecurity is concerned with the protection of critical data and resources across the cyber space. In this module we would be studying a cross section of the different controls required to protect our information systems. We shall be looking at various methods to secure networks, applications, data and other components on our information systems. Knowledge and skills gained from this module would prepare you for roles such as cybersecurity analyst, network security engineer amongst other roles.
COM715 Cyber Security Management 2 In the modern world of billions of connected devices knowledge in Cybersecurity and skills in management of information confidentiality, integrity and availability become essential, as well as ability to identify security risks and carry out risk assessments and mitigation. Cybersecurity professional should identify vulnerabilities and threats and gain an appreciation of how these threats can materialise and the typical attacks that can be carried to exploit vulnerabilities in a system. This module also involve the wider personal, organizational and regulatory context in which information systems are used and the impact of this on how cybersecurity is implemented.
COM700 Professional Issues and Practices 2 Current requirements by industry for professional standards indicate that every software engineer and manager must be aware of and abide by those standards. However, just abiding by them is not sufficient. Everyone within the organisation must be looking towards improvement of both their own standards and company standards. It is also necessary to identify how factors in business might impact on standards accounting for Legal, Social, Ethical and Professional issues (LSEPI) and also regulatory consequences of action or inaction. The only way to achieve this is with a knowledge of current practice and a goal or some goals that could be achieved. The module will look at standards of practice, not as a set of static documents but as a dynamic set of potential achievements, as for example envisaged by TickITplus and CMMI.
COM710 Web Technologies 2 This module provides learners from non-computing backgrounds with a thorough grounding in web development. It will introduce students to the common client- and server-side scripting technologies used in the development of web applications, such as HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Students will combine the material learned with additional research to develop a realistic web application. As this is a master level module students will be required to supplement the material learned in class with their own research in web development techniques and security issues, and demonstrate the application of this research in the practical assessment.
COM726 Dissertation 2 The Research Project allows students to undertake a large scale independent piece of research which directly follows on from the Pilot Project that forms a part of the final dissertation. This project must fit in with the core area of your degree, and must be focused on the gathering and analysis of data in order to solve a research question. Such research question(s) must be solved by utilising the knowledge and skills acquired throughout the taught part of your course. Hence, it is imperative that core skills of your degree are evidenced with the research work.
